What "safe water" actually means.

Why It Matters

What untreated water quietly costs you.

Hard, high-TDS or contaminated water rarely shows up as one dramatic event. It shows up slowly — on your body, in your appliances, and across your maintenance budget.

🧴

Skin, hair & health

Hardness above ~120 ppm is commonly linked to hair fall, dry skin, dandruff and irritation. High fluoride causes fluorosis; high nitrate endangers infants; bacteria cause waterborne disease.

⚙️

Appliance damage

Limescale clogs geysers, washing machines, dishwashers and solar heaters — cutting efficiency, raising power bills and forcing early, expensive replacements.

🚰

Fixtures & plumbing

White chalky deposits coat taps, showerheads and tiles. Scale narrows pipes over time, reducing flow and pressure throughout the building.

🫧

Soap & detergent waste

Hard water refuses to lather. You spend more on soap, shampoo and detergent — and still get residue on skin, hair and clothes.

💸

Hidden running costs

Scaling, corrosion, repeated repairs and shortened appliance life add up to far more than a one-time treatment system would have cost.

🏢

Institutional risk

For apartments, hospitals, schools and industry, non-compliant water means equipment downtime, dissatisfied residents and avoidable liability.
